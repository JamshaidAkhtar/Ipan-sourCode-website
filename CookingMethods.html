Cooking Methods

There are many ways to cook vegetables. The healthier options are stir-frying, microwaving and steaming because they ensure more nutrients remain intact. The guide ‘Preparing Vegetables’ included with this newsletter suggests different cooking methods for many vegetables. Stir-frying vegetables is quick. Vegetables can be cooked without water and with only a small amount of oil. Cut vegetables Place a small amount of oil into wok or large saucepan When sizzling, add onions and cook for 2 minutes Add the rest of the vegetables, stir and cook for 3–5 minutes. When the vegetables are a little softer, but still crunchy with vibrant colour, you may like to add a small amount of salt-reduced soy sauce and stir for 1 minute. Ready to eat! Steaming vegetables is great, as vegetables are cooked over water and not boiled in it so nutrients don’t leach out. Cut vegetables Boil a small amount of water in a saucepan (2 cups) Place a perforated steaming basket over the water Place vegetables in the basket Cover and cook for 5–8 minutes Ready to eat!
