Australian Dietary Guidelines

ShopSmart 4 Health aims to show you how to shop for and prepare healthy and affordable meals all year round.

  • plenty of fruit and vegetables (including legumes such as
  • chickpeas, lentils and beans), breads, pasta, rice, noodles and
  • cereals (preferably wholegrain)
  • moderate amounts of reduced fat milk, yoghurt and cheese,
  • lean meats and poultry, fish, eggs and nuts
  • small amounts of foods such as chocolate, chips, soft drinks
  • and fast foods on occasion, if at all
  • drinking plenty of water
  • Not all recipes are made equal. Changing a recipe into a healthier (and cheaper)
  • one is a really important and useful skill. The key things to remember are:
  • Remove, Reduce, and Replace (the 3 R s).
  • Remove: any extra salt, oil, fat or sugar.
  • Reduce: the amount of meat and ingredients high in salt and fat. Use low-salt and low-fat varieties. Using less meat is also cheaper.
  • Replace: with vegetables, legumes, fruit, herbs and spices.
