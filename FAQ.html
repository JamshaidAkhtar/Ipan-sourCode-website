  • Question: Aren’t fruit and vegetables expensive?
  • This is a common belief but in fact you can save money when you buy more fruit and vegetables, instead of packaged and ready-to-eat meals and snacks. Compare the cost of fresh fruit with that of fruit snacks per kilogram.
  • Question: What about tinned and frozen fruit and vegetables? Are they as nutritious as fresh?”
  • The good news is that tinned and frozen fruit and vegetables are just as nutritious as fresh ones, but make sure you look out for the following at the supermarket:

    • Tinned fruit – tinned in natural juice, not syrup; e.g. tinned peaches, apricots, pears, mango
    • Tinned vegetables – ‘salt-reduced’ or ‘no added salt’; e.g. tinned corn, peas, tomatoes, asparagus
    • Frozen fruit – e.g. frozen mixed berries
    • Frozen vegetables – without sauces e.g. frozen mixed vegetables, corn, spinach, beans, peas
    • Tinned legumes – without added salt e.g. chickpeas, butter beans, baked beans
  • Question: What are some simple ways I can add more fruit and vegetables into meals and snacks I prepare?”
  • Start with simple recipes with lots of fruit and/or vegetables like the ones in these newsletters!
  • Question: My family don’t like fruit and vegetables. What can I do?
  • Include your family in the meal planning process:
    • • If you take your children shopping with you, explain to them what you are doing and get them involved in finding the fruit or vegetables that you need.
    • • Make healthier versions of convenience or fast foods at home (home-made pizza can be packed full of vegetables)!
    • • Make sure each person gets their favourite healthy choice at least once a week.
    • • Try to introduce children to new foods as often as possible.
  • Question: I don’t like the taste of fruit and vegetables, What can I do?”
  • There are many different types of fruit and vegetables – they don’t all taste the same! Try new ones – there may be some that you like better than others, but it’s usually just a matter of getting used to the taste.
  • Question: I’m having guests over for dinner. What can I make that will include fruit and vegetables?”
  • Entertaining family and friends is an important and enjoyable part of life. Encourage others to eat a variety of fruit and vegetables at your next social occasion. All it takes is planning, enthusiasm and a few ideas (and it can all be done on a budget).
  • Question: How do I stay motivated when I’m getting bored cooking with vegetables?”
  • Here are a few suggestions of what vegetables work well together and how you can cook them:
    • Roasting- Pumpkin, Potatoes, Sweet potato, Red onion.
    • Stir-fries - Asian greens (eg. Bok choy), Broccoli, Snow peas, Baby corn, Red capsicum, Green beans, Carrots, Cauliflower.
    • Casseroles/pasta sauces Carrots - Mushrooms, Celery, Zucchini, Leeks, Tomatoes, Parsnips, Onion.
    • In sandwiches, wraps and burgers -Green salad leaves (eg. Rocket, Cos lettuce), Beetroot, Avocado, Capsicum, Red onion, Tomato, Mushrooms, Carrot.
    • Grilling/BBQ Eggplant -Zucchini, Onions, Asparagus, Mushrooms, capsicum, squash.
